Activity since: 1948


The Foundation stone of Muri Refinery was laid in January 1945. Muri plant is situated on the bank of Subarnarekha in Chota Muri village of Ranchi district in Jharkhand (65kms east of Ranchi town). Commissioned in 1948, the Muri alumina plant was India’s first alumina refinery under Indian Aluminium Company, Limited (Indal). HINDALCO acquired Indal effectively from March 7th2005 & took over Muri unit. Over the years, through various stages of expansion Muri Hydrate production capacity has been increased in several stages from 4KTPA in 1948 to a present capacity of 450 KTPA. The plant incorporates Bayer’ s process for production of alumina from bauxite ore. Present plant is operating at 330 KTPA based on the available bauxite quality & serve customers in aluminium, refractories, ceramics, water treatment, power and plastic industries. The Muri Works of Hindalco consists of an Alumina complex (450 KTPA) and also a Co-Generation Power Plant (2 X 15 MW) for manufacture of Standard Hydrate and Calcined Alumina. Total area of Muri works (including colony and red mud ponds) is 334.2 acres.

Energy Conservation Achievements 1. Reduction of 11 ata steam consumption in digester by Max HT usage

Before: Heat exchanger (regenerative) heat transfer co-efficient used to decrease sharply due to sodalite scaling inside tubes & hence average outlet temperature of Heat exchanger train decrease by appox. 4 to 5 Deg. C within 10 - 15 days of operation leading to poor heat recovery.
After: Due to sodalite scale inhibitor, heat transfer coefficient remains constant even after 60 – 70 days of operation, hence constant outlet temperature of Heat exchanger train and efficient heat recovery.
Coal Saved in FY-15 : 6267 Tonne
Savings in FY-15 : Rs. 269.88 Lacs
Investment : Rs. 52 Lacs

2. Optimization of Boiler Feed pumps Operation through de-staging


Before: 2 Boiler feed pumps (7 stage centrifugal) were operated initially and discharge pressure used to be in the range of 88 to 90 Kg/cm2).
After: Only one pump(6 Stage after one stage removal) is operated to cater total flow requirement of plant with discharge pressure of about 66 to 69 Kg/cm2)
Electricity savings in FY-15 : 2367.36 MWH
Savings in FY-15 : Rs. 89.96 Lacs
Investment : Rs. 44.04 Lacs
